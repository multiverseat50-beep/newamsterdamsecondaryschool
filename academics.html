Streams of Study

At the end of Grade 9 (Third Form), students are placed into one of the school's streams based on their academic performance, interests, career aspirations and the availability of places.

Arts and Humanities

Students pursue subjects in languages, literature, history, social sciences and the creative arts.

Natural Sciences

Designed for students interested in Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Mathematics and science-based careers.

Business Studies

Focuses on Accounting, Economics, Office Administration, Entrepreneurship and other business-related subjects.

Technical and Vocational Education and Training

  • Industrial Technology Option
  • Agricultural Science Option
  • Home Economics Option

General Stream

Provides students with a balanced programme of academic and practical subjects suited to a broad range of interests and career pathways.

Structure of the CAPE Associate Degree Programme

The CAPE Associate Degree programme combines General Education, Core, Supporting and Elective courses to provide students with a well-rounded academic qualification.

General Education Courses (Compulsory) Core Courses (Based on Specialisation) Supporting Courses Elective
  • Caribbean Studies
  • Communication Studies
  • Integrated Mathematics
 At least four CAPE Units relevant to the selected area of specialisation. At least two single units selected from the following CAPE categories:
  • Humanities and Fine Arts
  • Natural, Physical and Applied Sciences
  • Social and Behavioural Sciences
 One additional CAPE unit may be selected from any CAPE subject not already included in the General Education, Core or Supporting course requirements.
